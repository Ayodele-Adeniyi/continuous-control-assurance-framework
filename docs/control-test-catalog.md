# CCAF Control-Test Catalog

This catalog is the practitioner work program for Version 1.3.1. Each risk states what could have gone wrong. Each control statement describes the intended state of the environment. Each procedure identifies the records inspected or the check reperformed and the condition the procedure is designed to determine. The procedures identify conditions for investigation; they do not, by themselves, establish a control deviation, deficiency, misconduct, compliance failure, or financial loss.

All 20 tests are detective control-testing procedures. Their default type is **Detective; automated inspection/reperformance**. This describes the CCAF procedure, not necessarily the classification of the institution's underlying control.

## Source-evidence prerequisite

Before relying on a completed test, teams inspect the queries, reports, filter parameters, period selections, field mappings, row counts, control totals, and approvals used to generate the extract to determine whether the supplied records are complete and accurate for the purpose of the procedure. The input hash manifest preserves file identity after extraction; it does not establish completeness and accuracy. The source-assurance record identifies unresolved evidence requirements.

## How to read an evaluation

- **Completed:** the required records and analytical preconditions were present. The exception count and rate may be interpreted only for the supplied eligible population.
- **Not Evaluable:** a stated precondition was not met. No exception rate is calculated, and the result may not be described as a clean test.

The bracketed terms below are tailoring points. Teams replace them with institution-approved policy names, thresholds, periods, calendars, role definitions, source systems, and escalation requirements before operational use. Review-priority labels are demonstration settings for ordering follow-up; they are not institutional risk ratings.

## Evidence and conclusion protocol

For every completed test, teams retain sufficient appropriate evidence, including the approved configuration; queries and reports used to generate each extract; source-assurance record; data-quality results; evaluation status; eligible population; exception records; rule version; input hashes; reviewer disposition; and supporting records.

Teams use the following conclusion sequence:

1. **Automated exception:** the coded condition was met.
2. **Investigated condition:** a reviewer inspected the relevant records and inquired of responsible personnel.
3. **Confirmed deviation:** the reviewer determined that the relevant control requirement was not performed as designed.
4. **Deficiency or finding:** authorized assurance, risk, compliance, or control owners evaluated the deviation's significance under the institution's methodology.

CCAF produces the first item and structures evidence for the remaining steps. It does not make the later determinations automatically. For rollforward, teams determine whether there have been changes to the code, configuration, source logic, or control environment that could affect consistent operation since the procedure was tested.
